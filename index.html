About Me

i am a senior at university of michigan, studying statistics and computer science. i am currently a developer at feast, innovating the ways in which architecture students work with spatial data. on the side, i spend my time playing around with data visualizations, and dreaming up creative technologies

in a different life i did research in the mathematics department exploring origamis, in a media lab researching algorithmic literacy, and in an hci group building up simulations for the ways in which humans form teams.

i am also a writer of short stories, and creative nonfiction, who is still trying to gain the courage to publish her pieces (pronouns:she/her/hers). when i start getting too many ideas, you can find me making a mess with gouache or practicing yoga.

My Thoughts, My Creative Process

I have so so many question, so thought I might leave some of them here so more people could ponder them.

How should our technology practices change with the 6th extinction the world is going through? How can we develop data visualisations which lead people to action, when the current research on the field shows users don't really interact with them? How can we remain playful in our creation processes, while also looking the current social issues we face straight in the eye through the technologies we build?


I have always been a perfectionist, but technology and coding has helped me regain my comfort with prototyping. This website will forever remain imperfect, because it will always be one of my playgrounds. So thank you for visiting my little playground!

With all the data viz I create around climate change, I also write a newsletter on all my thoughts at the intersection of creativity and climate change solutions.
